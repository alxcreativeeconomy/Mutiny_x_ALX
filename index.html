Got Questions?

Is this a real job or just an internship?
This is a real paid professional engagement, NOT an internship. You'll be creating content for real artists with real audiences. Your work will be seen by millions of people. You'll be compensated for your work, and top performers will be offered full-time positions with Mutiny.
How long is it?
60-90 days (2-3 months).
Is it paid work?
Yes, a stipend/compensation is included during the pilot.
Who pays my salary ALX or ACA/Mutiny?
Mutiny will handle all salary payments and employment terms.
Do I have to be based in Johannesburg?
Yes, training will be conducted at the ALX JHB Hub.
How competitive is the selection process?
Very competitive. We're selecting 10-15 people from a large applicant pool. We're looking for a combination of basic skills, cultural fluency, coachability, and genuine passion for African music. Your portfolio matters, but so does your attitude and potential.
Can I apply for multiple roles?
Yes, but choose a primary role that best matches your strengths. If you have skills in multiple areas, mention it in your application. However, we'll place you in the role where you'll have the most impact.
Do I need specific equipment or software?
You'll need a computer and stable internet connection. For specific roles, you may need access to design/editing software (Adobe Creative Suite, Canva, CapCut, etc.). If you're selected, we'll provide guidance on tools and resources.
Is there any cost to participate?
No. There is absolutely no cost to apply or participate. You will be PAID for your work. This is a professional engagement, not a course you pay for.
What if I don't have experience?
We are looking for potential, hunger and coachability over perfection.
What if I don't have a portfolio yet?
That's okay! We're looking for potential, not perfection. If you don't have professional work, create sample projects for the artists (Xowie, Yonda, or Stacy). Make a mock social media post, write a sample bio, or design a concept album cover. Show us you have the hunger and creativity.
What is a Portfolio? (Tips inside)

We aren't looking for perfection; we’re looking for potential, hunger, and cultural fluency. We want to see that you understand the "vibe" of African artists and can meet a deadline. Your 3–5 examples of work should prove you have the basic skills to start creating immediately.

If you've never done a portfolio, here are some tips:

  • Social Media Creators: Submit links to high-performing Reels, TikToks, or a thread of 3-5 static posts that show you understand Gen Z trends.
  • Video Editors: Provide a link to a folder or YouTube/Vimeo/TikTok video you edited (Music videos or BTS preferred).
  • Graphic Designers: Upload a PDF or link to a folder containing album art, social media carousels, or promotional posters.
  • Copywriters: Submit a document with 3-5 examples of captions, bios, or a "mock" press release for an artist.
  • Data Analysts: Provide a screenshot or PDF of a basic report, spreadsheet, or trend analysis you've conducted.
  • Community Managers: Show us 3-5 screenshots of you engaging in a community (Discord, WhatsApp, or Social Comments) where you helped grow the vibe. Or your most engaged comments.
What's the best way to stand out?
Be authentic. Show genuine passion for African music and culture. Submit a strong portfolio (or create sample work if you don't have one). Tell us specifically which artist you're excited to work with and WHY. We're looking for hunger, creativity, and coachability, not just technical skills.
Did my app actually go through?
If you saw the "We’ve received your submission..." screen after hitting submit, we’ve got you.
When will I know if I’m shortlisted?
We move FAST. Our team will review all applications and reach out to the boldest candidates within one week of application to schedule assessments & interviews. If you haven't heard from us by then, it means we’ve moved forward with other candidates for this specific pilot.
How does the interview work?
If you're shortlisted, we’ll schedule your challenge assessment and telephonic call. We’ll talk about your passions, your chosen role, and how you handle feedback. We want to see if you’re ready to be part of the "modern digital street team". The agency will conduct final interviews.
What opportunities exist beyond this?
The skills and experience you gain are transferable to any role in digital marketing, social media, content creation, community management, or the music industry. You'll also have access to ACA Studios' network, which includes artists, producers, labels, and brands across Africa.
What happens after the pilot ends?
Top performers will be offered full-time positions with Mutiny. Performance is evaluated based on content quality, engagement metrics, work ethic, and cultural fit. Even if you're not offered a full-time role, you'll have real portfolio work and industry connections that will help your career.
